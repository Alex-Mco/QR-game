Vertebrate Land Invasion

In the late Devonian period, vertebrates such as Tiktaalik and Acanthostega evolved legs that allowed them to colonize the land. Fish had already evolved lungs in response to oxygen-depleted ocean conditions and to maintain buoyancy in the water, but other adaptations such as neck mobility and hard-shelled eggs were also critical to fully colonizing the new land environments.
